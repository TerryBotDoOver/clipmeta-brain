# Drone Footage for High-End Golf Courses

> Status: Raw idea, not validated
> Captured from Levi: 2026-04-11 (after golfing with Pete and Dennis)

## The Idea
High-end golf courses have screens/iPads on their newer golf carts that show animated overviews of each hole. Levi (a certified [[life_state|Part 107 drone pilot]]) could capture real drone footage of every hole from multiple angles. Courses could replace or supplement the animated overviews with actual bird's-eye-view drone footage. Players in the cart get a much better preview of the hole they're about to play.

## Why this could work
- Real footage > generic animations for serious golfers
- Levi already has the gear, the pilot certification, and the editing chops (this is his existing creative practice)
- Premium courses are willing to pay for premium experiences
- One-time capture (per course) → recurring license or upfront fee
- Could be expanded: weather variations, seasonal changes, drone footage for marketing/website too

## Why it might not work
- Premium courses already have polished animations from established providers (need to know who supplies these and what they charge)
- Drone restrictions over occupied courses — need to film when course is closed or empty (early mornings, off-season)
- FAA Part 107 has specific rules around flying near people, even with the certification
- Courses may have existing exclusive contracts with media providers
- Hardware integration: the cart screens need to actually be able to play custom video files. May require software cooperation from the cart system vendor
- Sales cycle to golf courses is probably long and relationship-based
- Need to figure out: do you sell to the course directly, or to the cart hardware/software vendor as a content partner?

## Open questions to research
1. Who currently makes the cart screen systems and the animations? (Visage, GPS Industries, ClubCar Tempo, others)
2. What do those companies charge courses and what's the content licensing model?
3. Are any of those vendors open to third-party content?
4. What FAA rules apply to filming over a private golf course (PROC, controlled airspace concerns)?
5. What's the typical premium course's annual marketing/tech budget?
6. Could this start as a marketing video service for the course's website/social before pitching the in-cart use case?
7. Do any nearby premium courses already have great hole-flyover videos? If so, who made them?

## Next concrete steps (when Levi has time)
