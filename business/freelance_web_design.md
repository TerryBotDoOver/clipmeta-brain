# Freelance Web Design Service

## What it is
A freelance side-service where [[levi|Levi]] takes on small WordPress / website jobs from people in his network. Terry handles the actual work (edits, content, plugin setup, etc.) while Levi manages the client relationship and pricing.

The first known client was a friend who needed WordPress edits on a SiteGround-hosted site. The current known active engagement is [[true_fence_florida|True Fence Florida]].

## Pricing guide (per Terry's notes)
| Job size | Price range |
|---|---|
| Simple edits | $50-150 |
| Medium changes | $150-400 |
| Major redesigns | $400-1,000+ |

## How it works
1. Client tells Levi what they need (verbally or via screenshots)
2. Levi gets pricing approved
3. Levi gets login credentials (wp-admin, hosting access)
4. Terry does the actual work
5. Levi reviews and delivers
6. Levi invoices

## What Terry needs from each new client
- wp-admin login (or hosting collab access)
- Site URL
- List of specific edits (screenshots preferred)
- Approval before going live

## How this fits into Levi's business mix
This is the smallest of [[levi|Levi]]'s ventures by revenue, but it has the lowest startup cost (no inventory, no infrastructure, no API spend) and the highest leverage per hour (Terry does the actual work).

## Honest pushback
Be careful with this one:
- Service businesses don't scale unless you productize them or build a team
- Each new client adds overhead that can't be automated (relationship, scoping, billing)
- Best use of this: ad-hoc income from existing network, NOT a product to grow into

If [[levi|Levi]] starts spending real time on this, the question becomes: is this hour better spent on [[clipmeta|ClipMeta]] growth (which has way more ceiling)? Probably yes. Use this to bring in cash for friends-of-friends, but don't market it.
