## Third session — cycle shuffle + gpt-image-1 thumbnails + overnight runner (late night, rolled into 4/17)

Levi pushed back hard on three fronts after seeing the first-draft setup. All three landed:

### 1. Cycle-based shuffle (guarantees, not heuristics)

His rule: "I never ever want to see two back to back clips together, but I do want to make sure that I see all of the videos play before it starts repeating them."

Old algorithm (`shuffle_clips`): flat shuffle of all N×K positions, then repair violations. Could NOT guarantee no-back-to-back at low clip counts — I'd relaxed the config to `hard=1` which actually permits back-to-back. Not what he asked for.

New algorithm `shuffle_clips_cycles()` in `pipeline/shuffle.py`:
- Build playlist as sequence of cycles. Each cycle = permutation of all N unique clips.
- Boundary guarantee: last clip of cycle K ≠ first clip of cycle K+1. Swap the first clip of the new cycle with a later position if that would create a duplicate at the boundary.
- Best-effort source-adjacency softener inside each cycle (Akaka_14 not next to Akaka_15), but never fails the run if infeasible.

Proven on 396Hz (68 clips × 36 cycles = 2448 positions trimmed to 2400): **zero literal back-to-back violations.** Proven on 174Hz (102 clips × 24 cycles) and 528Hz (21 clips × 115 cycles). Works regardless of clip count.

Wired into `run.py` (main pipeline) and `api/main.py` (dashboard preview). Old `shuffle_clips()` kept for backward compat and existing tests.

### 2. OpenAI gpt-image-1 thumbnail generator

Pulled the ClipMeta OpenAI key from Dell's `.env.local` via SSH, saved to `C:\StillFrequency\secrets\.env`. Installed `openai` + `python-dotenv`.

New module `pipeline/thumbnail_ai.py`:
- Call `gpt-image-1` at high quality, size 1536x1024, with a per-project photorealistic background prompt. Prompt injection includes "no text/letters/numbers/logos in the image" so the model leaves room for overlay.
- Center-crop to 16:9, resize to 1280x720.
- Apply bottom-gradient darkening (55-100% y, 75% intensity) for legibility.
- Pillow text overlay with the Still Frequency brand palette:
  - Top right: `STILL FREQUENCY` wordmark, Soft Gold #C9A96E, semi-transparent
  - Bottom left: large frequency number (170pt), white with soft-gold outer glow
  - Subtitle (44pt, white) 30px below the frequency
  - Strip (26pt, gold) at the bottom: `10 HOURS  ·  {THEME LABEL}`
- A/B variants: variant A leans cinematic golden hour, variant B leans moody dramatic twilight. Same prompt, different flavor injection.

First attempt had the `RELEASE FEAR` subtitle getting chewed by the "Hz" glow. Caught and fixed before the full batch ran — reduced glow radius 10→6, increased freq-to-subtitle gap 12→30px, computed layout from the bottom up so spacing is deterministic.

- **174Hz Kilauea Earth**: glowing lava crater with rising smoke against dark night sky. Deep navy + warm orange, strong contrast.
